## Design Context

### Users

Product engineers, designers, game developers, and indie teams choosing interaction sounds for web, mobile, SaaS, education, media, and game interfaces. They need to audition a coherent sound language quickly, switch its personality without rewriting event logic, and ship tiny assets with clear licensing.

### Brand Personality

Quiet, tactile, playful. Pips should feel like a beautifully labeled box of tiny physical switches: precise enough for a developer tool, warm enough for a consumer app, and surprising without becoming noisy.

### Aesthetic Direction

A light, editorial sound laboratory inspired by compact studio equipment, printed specimen books, and the direct manipulation of Suno's audio workspace. Warm paper-toned surfaces, dark ink, one vermilion accent, crisp waveform marks, and asymmetrical composition. Avoid generic dark AI gradients, glass panels, equal-sized card grids, decorative waveform clutter, and fake audio metrics.

### Design Principles

1. Let people hear the product within one click.
2. Organize by interaction meaning first and sonic personality second.
3. Make density feel calm through typographic hierarchy and generous rhythm.
4. Treat motion and sound as feedback, with reduced-motion and mute controls always available.
5. Show real file sizes, durations, formats, and code rather than marketing abstractions.
